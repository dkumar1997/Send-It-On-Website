Opportunities

Our volunteers, leaders, and executives are the heart of the organization. Their roles and responsibilities are essential to the well-being and smooth functioning of SEND IT ON’s operations. Below is a non-exhaustive list of the roles and current opportunities at SEND IT ON. The myriad of opportunities that we provide is our promise to you. Note, if there is a “2” by the position name, we are seeking two individuals for that position. Please assume the availability of one position otherwise. If you are interested or have any questions, please visit our sign up page or contact us. We look forward to having you join our family!

Mentors

  • Mentors develop positive relationships with mentees
  • Mentors provide learning opportunities and share information that may be useful to the mentee
  • Mentors invest time and provide a one-year commitment to their mentee
  • Mentors attend the SEND IT ON personal growth sessions
  • Mentors give full attention to their mentee while meeting with them

Representatives

  • Represent SEND IT ON through communicating SEND IT ON initiatives within faculties (if in school), companies, through social media accounts, and the general public
  • Brings forth ideas and feedback from the public

Executive Positions

PROJECT MANAGER

  • Leads project in selected global community
  • Ongoing communication with global partner and mentors working within the designated global community
  • Manages issues and risks
  • Is the point of call for such project
  • Reports to other members of the executive team on the progress of the mentor/mentee relationships
  • Serves as a voice for the mentors within the project
  • Manages handlings of the project

VICE PRESIDENT COMMUNICATIONS

Media Director

  • Creative management of social media accounts
  • Innovative approaches to website design and development
  • Design of posters, logos, and other print materials
  • Works closely with project planners to support campaigns
  • Actively promotes participation activities within the club
  • Formulates strategies to increase meaningful social media interactions

Meeting Director (2)

  • Works with co-founder to create personal growth webinars and sessions for SEND IT ON’s international partners (community members and youth) and mentors
  • Leads zoom webinars and meetings in collaboration with other members
  • Collaborates with members of the executive team to plan events, fundraising opportunities
  • Collaborates with VP Communications, Media Director in the designing of project plans, posters, and print materials for sessions and meetings

Mentor Coordinator (2)

  • Conducts meeting sessions and interviews with potential volunteers
  • Maintains admissions database
  • Collaborates with VP Communications Media Director to create content discussing SEND IT ON enrollment
  • Preparing individual applicant folders for review processes
  • Monitor admitted/registered yield and provide proactive follow-up with members in achieving enrollment goals

VP FUNDRAISING

  • Collaborates with other members of the executive board to create and organize events and activities that enhance educational, social, and professional goals and interests of volunteers and global partners
  • Evaluates the successes and challenges of events to make improvements or changes
  • Works closely with VP finances to ensure budgets are followed and documented

VP EXTERNAL

  • Represent SEND IT ON to local, national, and international organizations
  • Develops creative presentations for potential sponsors
  • Works with VP Communications to create a sponsorship strategy
  • Strategizes with executive board about innovative streams of development for SEND IT ON

VP FINANCE

  • Oversees club finances
  • Strategies with VP Fundraising about financials
  • Ensures that financial controls are maintained and that budgets for individual events/projects are approved by the board
  • Creates annual financial reports

VP INTERNAL

  • Publish the organizations event newsletter to general members
  • Maintenance of club documents including volunteer hours, board member meeting hours
  • Communicates information to members, volunteers, and global partners within an appropriate amount of time
  • Organizes club documents and facilitates administrative processes

Advocacy Director

  • Maximizes progressive governing agenda at the local, national, and international level
  • Develop strategies and negotiates solutions for policy issues important to the non-profit sector and national relations
  • Research, monitor, and analyze local, national, and international issues
  • Organize and engage members, non-profits, and policy makers in public policy efforts
  • Manage and produce web content, position statements, and research reports for members and other key stakeholders
  • Educate regarding the scope and impact of policy on local, national, and international level
  • Helps develop policy priorities and strategies
